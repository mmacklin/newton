Kernel Fixes: Newton Main to All Fixes

Newton Main uses the original VBD kernel code: log-barrier self-contact (H = k2/d2, unbounded), c = kd · ke contact damping (16,000x overdamped), and zero friction Hessian at rest. At 4 substeps this combination is catastrophically unstable (NaN on all seeds).

Fixes are applied progressively:

  1. Quadratic SC penalty — E = k/2·(r−d)2 with constant Hessian H = k. Eliminates the unbounded eigenvalues near close contact.
  2. Absolute damping — c = kd instead of kd · ke. Reduces damping Hessian eigenvalue by 6,250x.
  3. Friction Hessian at rest — Correct limiting value 2/ε when tangential slip is zero, so the solver sees friction stiffness for resting contacts.

Friction Model: P/D kf Sweep + IPC Comparison

The Primal/Dual friction model (Macklin 2020) uses a linear spring with stiffness kf in stick and Coulomb in slip. Its Hessian is a clean constant kf in the stick regime, avoiding the dt-dependent scaling artifact of the IPC C1 ramp. Setting kf ≈ ke balances normal and tangential stiffness. This section sweeps kf values and compares against the IPC model.

Issues Fixed

Seven issues were identified and fixed to make VBD stable with self-contact at 4 substeps.

Kernel-Level Fixes (code changes)

#IssueSeverityFix
1Log-barrier self-contact (H = k²/d², unbounded) Critical — NaN Quadratic penalty (H = k, constant)
2Contact damping c = kd · ke (16,000× overdamped) High — 5× jitter Absolute damping c = kd
3Friction Hessian approximation not scale-invariant Medium P/D friction model implemented as alternative
4Zero friction Hessian at rest (u == 0) Low Evaluate limit: hessian_scale = 2/ε

Configuration Fixes (parameter changes)

#IssueSeverityFix
5Self-contact buffer overflow (65% VT, 78% EE overflowed at 16/20) Critical — divergence Increase buffers to 512/512 (max observed: 257 VT, 483 EE)
6Particle masses near zero (1e-6 kg, stiffness ratio 38M:1) High — solver diverges Clamp min mass to 0.01 kg (10g), giving stiffness ratio ~17:1
7Contact stiffness ke=1e5 (still 1000:1 ratio with mass clamp) High Reduce to ke = kf = 1e4

Key Insight: Buffer Overflow Was the Dominant Problem

With default buffer sizes (16 VT / 20 EE), the collision detector silently dropped contacts that didn't fit. Each iteration saw a different, incomplete subset of contact forces. This caused:

  • The solver to actively diverge — residual grew from 21 to 51 over 40 iterations
  • 80 iterations was worse than 10 (reduction 0.64 vs 0.17)
  • All visible self-interpenetration in videos

With 512/512 buffers (no overflow) + mass clamp (stiffness ratio 17:1 instead of 38M:1), the solver converges monotonically and the cloth comes to rest.

Methodology

Scenario

T-shirt (unisex_shirt.usd, 6436 vertices) dropped from randomized height (0.05–0.20m) with random rotation and lateral offset. Self-contact enabled (margin=2cm, radius=2mm). 4 substeps × 10 VBD iterations at 60 FPS. Contact buffers 512/512. Min particle mass 0.01 kg. 2× SSAA with Lanczos downsample.

Metric Definitions

  • Med RMS (m/s): Median per-frame RMS vertex velocity over frames 90–180 (resting phase).
  • Max RMS (m/s): Maximum per-frame RMS velocity in resting phase.
  • Med Disp (mm): Median per-frame max vertex displacement in resting phase, in millimeters.
  • Pops: Number of frames in resting phase where max vertex displacement exceeds 0.5mm.
  • Conv Ratio: Median ratio residual[last] / residual[0] across convergence samples (frames 100–170, first substep).
  • NaN Seeds: Number of seeds that produced NaN positions.
